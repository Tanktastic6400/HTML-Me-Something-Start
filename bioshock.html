Have you ever wondered what an isolated post-apocalyptic city would look like if it was 2000 leagues below the sea and also doubled as your worst nightmare? Look no further than at the city of Rapture. A city that was designed to be an isolated utopia, quickly ran into problems when ADAM was discovered, this was refined into a serum that gave people superhuman-like powers. This utopia didn’t last however, soon wealth disparages caused an all out war to start. People went insane, killed each other and the property value went down tremendously.

I don’t remember why I tried Bioshock, I do remember being scared out of my pants when I played the demo which was essentially the first 10 minutes of the game. What I think really stands out about the game is that it didn’t pull any punches like games I had played up to that point. It was dark, it was gritty, it was immersive in its horror. It really helped show what kind of dark art styles I enjoy. Also the story was just fantastic, it really keeps you guessing and as someone who doesn’t fall for plot twists easily, it really kept me guessing about what was going to happen next.
